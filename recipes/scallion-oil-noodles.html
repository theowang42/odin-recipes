Scallion Oil Noodles

Scallion oil noodles are simple but precise. Slowly fried scallions create a deep aroma, soy sauce builds the base, and hot noodles carry the glossy sauce.

Time25 min
Serves2
LevelEasy
StyleNoodles

Ingredients

  • 250 g fresh wheat noodles or dried noodles
  • 5 scallions, cut into long strips
  • 4 tablespoons neutral oil
  • 2 tablespoons light soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds
Tip: Fry the scallions slowly. The flavor comes from deep browning, not high heat.

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a small pan over medium-low heat. Add scallion strips.
  2. Fry slowly until the scallions turn deep golden and crisp at the edges.
  3. Turn off the heat. Stir in light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and sugar.
  4. Cook noodles according to package directions, then drain well.
  5. Toss hot noodles with the scallion oil sauce until evenly coated.
  6. Top with fried scallions and sesame seeds. Serve immediately.
