Sichuan comfort

Mapo Tofu

Mapo tofu is a spicy, glossy tofu dish built around chili bean paste, aromatics, and a savory sauce. The goal is contrast: soft tofu, rich sauce, fragrant heat, and a clean finish from scallions.

Time35 min
Serves2–3
LevelMedium
HeatHigh

Ingredients

  • 400 g soft or medium tofu, cut into cubes
  • 120 g ground pork or beef
  • 2 tablespoons doubanjiang chili bean paste
  • 1 tablespoon fermented black beans, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1 cup chicken stock or water
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water
  • Scallions and ground Sichuan peppercorns, to finish
Tip: Simmer the tofu gently. Stirring too hard will break the cubes.

Steps

  1. Bring a small pot of salted water to a gentle simmer. Add the tofu cubes for 1 minute, then drain carefully.
  2. Heat oil in a wok or skillet. Cook the ground meat until browned and slightly crisp.
  3. Add doubanjiang, black beans, garlic, and ginger. Stir-fry until the oil turns red and fragrant.
  4. Pour in stock, soy sauce, and sugar. Bring the sauce to a steady simmer.
  5. Add tofu and simmer for 5 minutes so the tofu absorbs the sauce.
  6. Stir in the cornstarch slurry in two additions until the sauce turns glossy and lightly thickened.
  7. Finish with scallions and ground Sichuan peppercorns. Serve hot with rice.
