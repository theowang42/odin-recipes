Mapo Tofu

Description

Mapo tofu is a Sichuan-style tofu dish built around soft tofu, savory bean paste, chili oil, and a numbing finish from Sichuan peppercorn. It is rich, spicy, and best served over plain steamed rice.

This version keeps the technique simple: bloom the aromatics, build a short sauce, simmer the tofu gently, and thicken at the end so the cubes stay soft instead of broken.

Ingredients

  • 450 g soft or medium tofu, cut into cubes
  • 120 g ground pork or beef
  • 1 tablespoon doubanjiang, or spicy fermented bean paste
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon minced ginger
  • 1 cup chicken stock or water
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 teaspoon ground Sichuan peppercorn
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Chopped scallions, for garnish

Steps

  1. Bring a small pot of water to a simmer and warm the tofu cubes for 2 minutes, then drain carefully.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the ground meat and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Add doubanjiang, garlic, and ginger. Stir for about 1 minute until the oil turns red and fragrant.
  4. Pour in the stock, soy sauce, and sugar. Stir to combine and bring the sauce to a gentle simmer.
  5. Add tofu and simmer for 4 to 5 minutes, moving the pan gently instead of stirring aggressively.
  6. Add the cornstarch slurry a little at a time until the sauce lightly coats the tofu.
  7. Finish with Sichuan peppercorn and scallions. Serve hot with rice.
